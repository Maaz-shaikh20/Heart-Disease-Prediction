Key Risk Factors

ST Segment Abnormalities

Changes in the ST segment on ECG are among the strongest indicators of coronary artery disease.

Exercise-Induced Angina

Chest pain during exercise indicates insufficient blood flow to the heart muscles.

Resting Blood Pressure

Elevated resting BP (>140 mmHg) is a major cardiovascular risk factor requiring management.

Maximum Heart Rate

Lower max HR during exercise may indicate cardiac dysfunction or poor exercise tolerance.

Cholesterol Levels

High cholesterol (>200 mg/dL) accelerates atherosclerosis and increases disease risk.

ST Depression (Oldpeak)

ST segment depression during exercise is a significant ischemia indicator.
